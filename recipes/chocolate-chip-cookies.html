Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

This recipe will be without a doubt your new go-to chocolate chip cookie recipe. This dough does not need to be chilled and can be mixed by hand! They are soft on the inside, yet cripsy and chewy on the outside.

Baking Tips

  1. Properly mesure the flour - There is good reason for this tip, this is the main reason why most cookie recipes usually fail. If there is too much flour the cookies will be too dry. I would recommend measuring with a food scale if possible or spoon the flour into the measuring cup and level off with a flat knife.
  2. The More Sugars the Merrier - This recipe uses both light brown sugar and granulated sugar. The molasses in the light brown sugar is what gives the cookies a soft, yet chewy texture. The granulated sugar is what gives the cookie the golden crispy ring around its edge (yum!)
  3. DO NOT OVERBAKE - You ALWAYS want to pull the cookeis out when they are golden brown with a pale center that looks slightly underdone. The cookies will continue to bake outside the oven and as they cool the will flatten which gives you the perfect texture in the middle!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cups all purpose flour, spooned and leveled
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 1/2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • flaky sea salt

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 F and line a large baking sheet with parchment.
  2. In a medium bowl, mix together the flour, baking soda, paking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, add the melted butter, brown sugar, and sugar and vigorously mix together for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Whisk in the egg and vanilla until smooth.
  5. Pour in the dry ingredients and fold until combined and then pour in the chocolate chips. Keep folding until the chocolate chips are completely blended in.
  6. Scoop about 5 cookies in a 2 oz cookie scoop onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes.
  7. The cookies are ready when they have a golden ring around the edge and are pale and puffed in the center.
  8. For a litte extra somthin' add a pinch of flaky sea salt on top of each cookie after pulling them out! Wait about 20 minutes ot until the cookies feel cool to eat. Enjoy!
